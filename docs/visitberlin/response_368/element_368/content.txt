            KINDL - Zentrum für zeitgenössische Kunst

                      

                    

                    
                
              The listed red-brick ensemble of the former Kindl brewery was built between 1926 - 1930 in a style reminiscent of German expressionism and a design of the architects Hans Claus & Richard Schepke. After the purchase of the German-Swiss couple Burkhard Varnholt & Salome Grisard in 2011 the space was refurbished for contemporary cultural productions. Depending on the individual exhibition plans there is one flat of the former Power House, the spectacular 20-metre-high Boiler House and the Brew House with its six enormous copper available for different events! The rooms and buildings are clearly characterised by their original use and can be rededicated appropriately today.
