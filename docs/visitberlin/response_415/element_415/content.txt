            Conference: Future electricity grid

                      

                    

                            
                        
                
            
The transformation of energy supply towards a decarbonized sustainable energy system is turning the traditional structure upside down, especially in the electricity sector. The power grid has an important integrative role to play here because, on the one hand, volatile renewable energies are integrated locally into the grid and, on the other hand, the transport routes for national and transnational use must be implemented in good time.The current transformation scenarios also show that the electrification of the transport and heating sectors is becoming increasingly important. The aim is to ensure acceptance among the population and also to find an economically viable solution through a suitable market and regulatory design. Our conference "Future electricity grids" addresses the issues surrounding electricity grids: What structures do we need in distribution and transmission grids? What are the stability criteria for the safe operation of the system in the future? How will our infrastructure change with regard to the digitization of the system? Which technologies are required for reliable monitoring and operational management of the energy system? What regulatory framework do we need for the energy market and the power grid? What are the appropriate price and allocation mechanisms to achieve a reliable framework for investors? Ideas and concrete proposals for technical solutions to the challenges are examined and discussed in political, research and industrial circles. Our congress will show what contribution individual technologies can make to a sustainable energy supply in the transformation process. Key technologies that industry and research are currently working on as part of the research programmes will also be discussed.
