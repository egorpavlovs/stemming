            Deutsches Historisches Museum

                      

                    

                    
                
              The 'Deutsche Historische Museum' is located in one of the most representative areas of Berlin, close to the legendary 'Museumsinsel' (Museum Island) and to the new government district directly beside the river Spree. On May 28th, 1695 the Elector Friedrich III of Brandenburg gave the orders to lay the foundation stone of the Berlin 'Zeughaus'. In 1987 the German Historical Museum was founded at the 'Zeughaus'. Its ideal location at the "Unter den Linden"-Boulevard makes it perfect for dignified, representative and high class events in the very center of Berlin.
