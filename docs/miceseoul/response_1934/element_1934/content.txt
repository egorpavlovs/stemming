								The innovativenon-verbal performance, Ballerina Who Loved a B-boy, as the name suggests,combines ballet with street dance.
Whether young or old,male or female, Ballerina Who Loved a B-boy releases the viewer from all socialbarriers, giving them a sense of camaraderie with their fellow spectators andmaking them realize they are part of a much greater whole. The uniqueperformance helps all find that which resides within, and the awakening of aninexhaustible energy.
The non-verbalperformance is easily accessible to international visitors and has been seenall over the world. At the Edinburgh Fringe Festival it was declared the bestof the 2,050 entries in 2007. Its successful long run of 40 days and 50 showson Broadway in 2008 prompted Broadway promoters to invite it back for apermanent run in the Time Square theater district.
