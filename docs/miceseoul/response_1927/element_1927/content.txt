								SamulNori is the name of a group of four dynamicmusicians dedicated to performing and preserving traditional Korean music anddance. The name also refers to the style of music they created. Since thesesuperb percussionists formed the group in 1978, SamulNori has sparked arenaissance in Korea’s music scene and garnered worldwide acclaim more than 40years.
The Korean words ‘Samul’ means ‘four things’ and ‘nori’means ‘to play.’ In the case of SamulNori, it refers to the four musiciansplaying and dancing with four percussion instruments. Founded by Kim, Duk-Soo,the group’s leader and master of the Jang-go(hourglass shape drum), SamulNorihas become the leading traditional Korean Performance group. The ensembleperforms in many configurations but usually tours as a quartet with Master Kimat the helm. The origins of their music can be traced to what is usuallyreferred to as farmers’ band music(Poongmul) and ceremonial music(Gut). It alsoincorporates the influences of folk and religious music and their intricaterhythms have become quite uniquely their own.
