        The National Museum of Korea is the nation’s main cultural and historical  repository, preserving and exhibiting items from prehistoric times to the early  20th Century. Since it first opened in 2005, the museum has received a wealth  of international acclaim, including being ranked the 9th most-visited museum  worldwide by the Art Newspaper and declared a “must-see place” by the Michelin  Guide, which also awarded it three stars.For visitors, the museum offers free one-hour guided tours in Korean, English,  Japanese, and Chinese on its major masterpieces. It also functions as a unique  venue for international conventions and receptions in its indoor and outdoor  spaces, notably including the welcome reception and working dinner for the G20  Seoul Summit 2010. Visitors can also attend fabulous world-class performances and concerts at  the complex’s Yong Theater or simply enjoy the afternoon in the tree-filled  park adjacent to the museum.TransportationFrom Incheon International Airport: 40~50 minSubway: Ichon station(Line 4, Jungang Line)Nearby hotels & attractionsWithin 3km: Hotel Capital, Grand Hyatt SeoulWithin 5km: Lotte City Hotel Mapo, Banyan Tree Club & Spa, Ramada     Hotel & Suites Namdaemun Seoul, Best Western New Seoul Hotel
