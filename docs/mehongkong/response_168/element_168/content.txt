                    Tai O Heritage Hotel

	
	
  

	
		
		
    
		
	
	
	

                  
                  
  
  
    
    
  
  
    
      
    
  


    
      Celebrate the splendour of local heritage

Near to the oldest fishing village in Hong Kong, the Tai O Heritage Hotel was once the Tai O Police Station. Built in 1902, it was declared a heritage building by the Antiquities Advisory Board in 2010.

This splendid, colonial-style building is now home to nine palatial rooms which are classically furnished to match this heritage. The two-storey hotel is ideal for small-scale events such as off-site executive meetings, brainstorm sessions and workshops.

For fine-dining, the hotel has a glass roof, open-view restaurant with panoramass of the South China Sea.

When meetings are done for the day, a walking tour or a sunset boat ride around the Tai O fishing village lets visitors take in the sights, sounds and charms of peaceful village living.

            How to Get There
            
            
              Walk for five minutes from the Tai O bus terminus to the Rope-drawn Ferry Bridge. Cross the bridge and walk along Tai O Market Street. Turn left to Shek Tsai Po Street and walk another 30 minutes to the street's end.
