                Introduction
                
                  Rich in history, yet designed for the needs of today, the Royal College of Physicians is an award-winning, modern building overlooking Regent's Park, with a private garden in the heart of London.

              Overview

              Designed by Sir Denys Lasdun and completed in 1964, it is a modernist masterpiece awarded small museum status and a grade 1 listing in recognition of its architectural importance.
                  The building has an atmosphere of space and light, with stylish, modern architecture and includes an impressive portfolio of purpose-designed facilities including; auditoriums, meeting and dining rooms, as well as exhibition areas, and a private garden ideal for events, barbecues and al fresco dining.
                  The facilities are further enhanced by numerous rare treasures, artefacts, rare books and manuscripts. We offer tailor-made guided historical tours, including anecdotes and stories about our collection.
                  This content has been supplied by Royal College of Physicians
