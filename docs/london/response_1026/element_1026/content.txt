              Overview

              Situated at the heart of London's vibrant South Bank cultural district, Southbank Centre comprises of the Royal Festival Hall, Queen Elizabeth Hall and Hayward Gallery.    The central riverside location further benefits from iconic London views and excellent transport links. 
                  Built as part of the Festival of Britain in 1951, the Royal Festival Hall is grade 1 listed and can host events from 20 - 2,500 guests.
                  Newly re-opened in Spring 2018, the Queen Elizabeth Hall will be able to host events for 200 - 916 delegates in it's two beautiful, wood-panelled auditoria.
                  For the first time ever, the world-renowned Hayward Gallery is available to hold private events including dinners and drinks receptions.  
                  For unique event spaces in an inspirational cultural venue with a central London location, look no further than Southbank Centre.
                  This content has been supplied by Southbank Centre Events
