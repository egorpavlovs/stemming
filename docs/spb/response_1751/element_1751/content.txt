                Saint Petersburg celebrated the Koryushka Festival 
            
            May 19 2015
        

        






        
            
                
                

The Koryushka Festival or smelt fish festival was held in Saint Petersburg on May 16-17. The event made happy its visitors bringing a lot of fried fishes, entertainments and a spring positive.

The Koryushka or smelt fish is the Saint Petersburg’s symbol like Hermitage and drawbridges. The first festival in honor of the Neva main commercial fish was held under the Decree of Peter I 300 years ago. The historical tradition was renewed only in 2003. From that time the festival is being held annually and always with surprises. The shark was hooked during fishing in 2007. Famous sculptor Grigoriy Pototskiy made the bronze artwork of koryushka in 2011.   

The picnic in the open air 2015 was held on a grander scale than previous. The program included the cooking and the degustation of General’s fish soup and the tradition Finnish fish soup, the competition of fishermen, the gigantic dominoes named The Fish. Among the events were concerts and the stocking action. The Koryushka was fried on 40 outdoors grills during the festival.
