                Managing risks to coastal regions will be discussed at EMECS’11 in Saint Petersburg
            
            August 16 2016
        

        




    
        
            
                
                
            
                
                
            
        
    



        
            
                
                The international conference “Managing risks to coastal regions and communities in a changing world, EMECS’11” based on Russian state hydrometeorological university will take place on August, 22-28 for the first time in Russia. The conference will be held under support of Saint Petersburg convention bureau and will be attended by over 300 delegates from all continents, representing 30 countries.

Conferences of the problems of coastal areas are held traditionally every three years in different countries all over the world. The upcoming conference will be the eleventh.  Choosing the venue EMECS'11 conference is due to the interests of the International Centre EMECS to the regions of the Russian Arctic seas and the Baltic Sea and the fact that Saint Petersburg is provided as the Russian center of the Arctic development research.

Conference topics cover a wide range of issues, such as coastal systems and their dynamics (from coast to water and from water to coast), coastal erosion and dynamical processes in the nearshore zone, climate change in the changing world and others.

The conference is organized by the International Center for Environmental Management of Enclosed Coastal Seas (International EMECS Center) and «Sea Coasts» Working Group of the RAS Council on the World Ocean, an organization support of the Conference is realized by Saint Petersburg convention bureau.

Special “Youth Session” will be organized within the programme of the Conference, there high school pupils and first-year universities students from different countries will work out their proposal on prospects of the coastal zone in a changing world.
