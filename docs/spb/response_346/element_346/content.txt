                    Saint Petersburg State University of Industrial Technologies and Design
                
            

        
            
                
                    
                
            

            
                
                    Saint Petersburg State University of Industrial Technologies and Design was established pursuant to the resolution of the Supreme Council of national economy of the USSR of 26 April 1930 as Leningrad Textile Institute which was named after S.M. Kirov by the Presidium of the Central Executive Committee of the Ussr on 31 August 1935. Nowadays the University holds the status of the Federal State Budgetary Educational Institution. Powers and duties of the institution establisher are performed by the Ministry of Education and Science of the Russian Federation.In 2015, the University celebrated its 85-th anniversary.Nowadays our University is a multidisciplinary educational complex that includes 2 higher schools, 18 Institutes and 2 colleges. University offers today a wide range of educational programmes for secondary, higher and additional vocational as well as for postgraduate education. At the moment the University trains specialists in 249 secondary vocational, undergraduate, postgraduate and professional development programmes in 14 major specialties. More than 16,000 students majoring in design, the humanities, engineering, economy and pedagogy study here. Nowadays there are about 16,000 students at SPbSUITD. High standards of professional education for the graduates are maintained by famous scientists and qualified university teachers. There are more than 300 doctors of science and 400 candidates of science, 10 honourary figures of science of the RF and more than 30 honourary figures of culture, higher education and members of creative unions – designers’ union and artists’ union.Scientific activity of the University is an integral part of the specialists’ training. The unity of educational and scientific processes is guaranteed by the participation of both teaching staff and students in research work.Development of international activities is considered to be an essential part of the University’s international strategy aimed at improving the quality of education and the position in the world universities rankings. Promotion of international activities also corresponds to the purposes and objectives of higher education in Russia, contributes to overcoming the isolation of higher education, encouraging its openness and integration into the world educational space.The international students’ community counts more than 600 students enrolled in the university from more than 20 countries including Baltic, the CIS and other states. Foreign students study in international groups together with Russian students. Educational programmes for international students, as well as for every SPbSUITD student, are designed according to the Federal State Educational Standard and curriculum approved by the university for each subject area.
