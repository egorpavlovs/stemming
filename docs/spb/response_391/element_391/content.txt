                                  A.S.Popov Central Museum of Communications offers unique opportunities for organizing business, protocol, educational, corporative and high life events and implementing different exhibition projects. The palace, in which the museum is housed, is an architectural landmark of the last quarter of the 18th century. The building was erected based on the project of the architect Giacomo Quarenghi and was intended for the famous statesman – Chancellor of the Russian Empire Prince A.A.Bezborodko. The museum is situated in the center of Saint Petersburg, near St. Isaac’s Square and is surrounded with historical monuments.Depending upon the format of the event, you can select one of multi-functional halls, each of which has its own individual style. The Great Stateroom is distinguished by historical decorative details in classicism style. The main interesting point of Atrium I is an authentic communication sputnik “Luch-15”. Atrium II is characterized by the interesting design of the exhibition in hi-tech style. The halls are equipped with projecting and computer devices and acoustic systems, it is possible to get connected to Internet and to set up facilities for synchronous translation.A corporative event taking place at the museum can be augmented with the general excursion throughout the museum halls, which will invite the participants to the amazing world of communication, or an excursion round the “Philatelic Treasury of Russia”, where the rarities from the National collection of stamps and postal stationery are preserved. It will offer your guests a possibility to enjoy interesting and useful rest during the pause between the sessions and will give a cognitive tinge to the evening event.
