                                  The Mihail Chemiakin Foundation was established in 2002 to promote cultural and academic programs. The Foundation aims to support the art of Russia, organize cultural exchange with other countries and aid young aritsts, photographers and musicians.The Foundation’s exhibition projects are based on the work of “Mihail Chemiakin’s Musee Imaginaire”. The Foundation conducts educational programs, providing access to the unique visual materials in Chemiakin’s collection – images culled from catalogues, books, illustrations, and photographs of museum and private collections. The Foundation has hosted exhibits dedicated to "The Sphere in Art”, “Images of Death in Art”, “Cries in Art”, “Monsters and Mythological Creatures”, “The Hand in Art”. An important component of each of these exhibitions is the organization of an open international competition and the inclusion in the exhibition of original works by contemporary artists, selected from submissions to the competition.Each exhition is accompanied by a series of events tied to the given theme – poetry readings, concerts, film festivals, and discussions.The Mihail Chemiakin Foundation works closely with the Museum of Oriental Art and its Maikop branch, where the thematic exhibitions are mounted with original works by artists of the Northern Caucasus.
