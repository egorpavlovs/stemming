	In the centre of the action!

	 
		communications
		Montréal international
					
    
    A friendly, attractive and multicultural city, Montréal bridges European charm with North American efficiency and comfort. Essentially, it’s the best of both worlds!
As for Montréal’s convention centre, the Palais des congrès, it is located in the middle of the city’s most vibrant districts.

 
Old Montréal

Old Montréal is sublimely artistic and enchanting, and also awash in heritage and history. Cobblestone streets that exude Paris, exceptional architecture like the Notre-Dame Basilica, a parade of renowned restaurants and designer boutiques, Old Montréal is unique, trendy, but above all, full of surprises!
 
 
 
Chinatown

Montréal’s Chinese community settled along De La Gauchetière street, near Saint-Laurent boulevard, back in 1860. Today, Chinatown’s narrow streets, architecture, typical signs and shops will transport you to Beijing! The people, the traditional arts and crafts, the exotic products, herbs, teas, and even the Oriental wellness centres, give this corner of Montréal a unique personality that will simply take you away.
 
 
 
Arts & Entertainment District

Home to Montréal culture in the heart of downtown, which is always bustling with activity! Festivals, movies, technology, fashion, comedy, the theatre and much more! This place is exciting and alive day or night, teeming with cultural events and activities. You’ll definitely want to visit Montréal’s Arts & Entertainment District.
 
 
 
International District

A showcase for the city’s and province’s “savoir-faire” and a platform for design, architecture and cultural events, the International District is a pleasant mix of modernism and classicism. The international scope of this area is magnified by the presence of major organizations such as the Palais des congrès de Montréal, the International Civil Aviation Organization, the World Trade Centre and the Caisse de dépôt et placement du Québec.
 
 
Business District

The city’s economic hub, Montréal’s business district combines iconic Victorian architecture with tall modern skyscrapers. The posh boutiques, Montréal’s underground city and the formidable corporate community make it a dynamic and prosperous area that radiates refinement.
