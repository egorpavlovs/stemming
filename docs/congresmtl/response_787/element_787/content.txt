	Expansion project: An update

	 
		communications
		Conférences, réunions et galas, Congrès
					
    
    The Palais des congrès de Montréal has been one of the city’s key economic players for 30+ years. After hosting 6,000 events and over 15 million visitors to date, the Palais looks forward to continuing to generate economic and intellectual spinoffs for Montréal and Québec as a whole.
As a result, the Palais’ various teams have gone on the offensive, and the numbers speak volumes! This past year alone, the Palais has added 388 new events to an already busy schedule. In only a few years, the occupancy rate will reach a critical point, which means the Palais will have to expand if it wishes to maintain its market share and continue to be an economic leader.
Targeting two city blocks near the Palais
The Québec Cabinet therefore proceeded in March to reserve over 12,000 sqm of land bordering the Palais to the North and West. The Québec government has taken this step to safeguard the Palais’ options for the future.
A review is underway!
The Palais now has a two-year mandate, renewable for another two years, to complete its review of the expansion project. The review process will obviously take into account the needs of the Healthcare District located nearby. At present, nearly 33% of the international conventions held at the Palais are related to the healthcare sector.
