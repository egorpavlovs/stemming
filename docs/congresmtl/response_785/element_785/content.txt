	Constellation of Great Montréalers: 2013 Inductees

	 
		communications
		Club des Ambassadeurs, Montréal international, Créneaux d’excellence
					
    
    In November 2012, as part of its 190th anniversary celebrations, the Board of Trade of Metropolitan Montréal unveiled a permanent art installation paying tribute to the members of the Academy who have contributed to the economic, cultural, scientific and social advancement of the Greater Montréal area.
This year, four new Great Montréalers saw their names immortalized on this living work of art that speaks to the achievements of men and women who are models of commitment and success.

The Academy is pleased to recognize the new inductees and their exceptional careers for their role in shaping our great city.
• Lise Watier, founder of Lise Watier Cosmetics and president of the Lise Watier Foundation. An entrepreneurial pioneer in Québec, she built the largest cosmetics company Québec has ever known.
• Kent Nagano, music director of the Orchestre symphonique de Montréal. The wealth of his experience and vastness of his repertoire have contributed extensively to the prestige and musical reputation of Montréal.
• Julie Payette, COO of the Montréal Science Centre and vice-president of the Canada Lands Company. The first Canadian to work aboard the International Space Station, she consolidated Montréal’s reputation in the field of astronomy and pointed the way to the stars for thousands of young Québecers.
• Pierre Bourgie helped raised awareness among Montréalers and Québecers about the importance of increasing funding to social and cultural organizations.
The bluish wall display features 127 points of light that pay tribute to the Great Montréalers honoured thus far, some of whom are members of the Palais des congrès Ambassadors Club!
• Mr. André Caillé                        • Dr. William Feindel
• Dr. Pavel Hamet                       • Mr. Bernard Lamarre
• Dr. Balfour M. Mount
Come discover the Constellation and a host of other works by Québec artists displayed both inside and outside the Palais des congrès de Montréal.
