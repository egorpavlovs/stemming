																																																			The Palais des 
congrès de Montréal is well known in the city for bringing together art 
and architecture in perfect harmony.
																																																		
																																																			On November 22, 2012,
 as part of its 190th anniversary celebrations, the Board of Trade of 
Metropolitan Montréal unveiled a permanent art installation paying 
tribute to the members of the Academy who have contributed to the 
economic, cultural, scientific and social advancement of the Greater 
Montréal area. The new Constellation of Great Montréalers represents a 
tradition of recognition that began in 1988. Housed inside the 
multicoloured glass exterior of the Palais, along the lobby of Place 
Riopelle, it speaks to the outstanding achievements of men and women who
 are models of commitment and success, some of whom are members of the 
Palais Ambassadors Club! It is also an opportunity for Montréalers and 
visitors to get to know the people who shape our city.
																																																		
																																																			The bluish wall 
display features 122 points of light that pay tribute to the Great 
Montréalers honoured thus far. This hi-tech art installation allows 
people to scan their smartphones over each point to retrieve information
 on the luminaries who have helped make Montréal such a dynamic city.
																																																		
																																																			Come discover the 
Constellation and a host of other works by Québec artists displayed both
 inside and outside the Palais des congrès de Montréal.
