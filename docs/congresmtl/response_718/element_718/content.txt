	Culti-Vert overview of 2015: Crops and experiments

	 
		communications
		Responsabilité sociale, Développement durable
					
    
    The warm weather is behind us, which means it’s time to turn our attention to assessing the kind of year the Culti-Vert vegetable garden we planted on the Palais rooftop in the summer of 2011 has had. Despite the inclement early summer weather, we harvested around 500 kg of fruit, vegetables and fine herbs – all ingredients that regularly find their way to the dishes Capital Catering prepares for the Palais’ guests.
Some of the additions this summer included bee plants on the 7th floor terrace, which helped spur more pollination and production by the bees from our 3 hives, responsible for the more than 30 kg of honey collected by Miel Montréal onsite.  Nice work! 
Also, we planted hops in climber planters together with Montréal Houblonnière. Too early to reap anything from this experiment, but it does teem with interesting possibilities for the coming summers.
The rooftop garden hosted numerous tours and film/photo shoots all summer long, including visits from urban faming summer students, which can only mean that urban agriculture is still a hot and trending topic.
