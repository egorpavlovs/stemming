	The Palais des congrès joins the “Québec Food on the Menu” initiative

	 
		communications
		Expérience culinaire
					
    
    The Palais des congrès de Montréal is proud to take part in the Aliments du Québec au menu (“Québec Food on the Menu”) initiative launched by Aliments Québec earlier this summer. The program recognizes restaurateurs and caterers that promote Québec food products on their menus and offers the opportunity to have 5 of their dishes officially certified with the Aliments du Québec (“Québec food”) seal. Certification requires that a minimum of 85% of the main ingredients come from Québec and all processing and packaging activities must be done in the province.
The pilot project was a natural fit with the Palais, whose mission includes the promotion of Québec artists and designers, and of local products and flavours. In fact, the Palais is the first and only convention centre to offer Aliments du Québec certified dishes..
Of course, when it comes to food, Palais executive chef Simon Devost-Dulude has always had a passion for local ingredients and Québec products. The menus he prepares for Capital Traiteur, the exclusive caterer at the Palais, even include ingredients that are harvested fresh from the Palais rooftop garden.
As part of the “Québec Food on the Menu” program, Chef Devost-Dulude has created five Aliments du Québec certified dishes that will allow Palais clients to savour the excellence of Québec products:
Nagano pork tenderloin with Arabica demi glaze
Served with old-fashioned mustard and dates, accompanied by parsnip puree, sautéed red cabbage and beets with sautéed peppers.

Roast saddle of Beauce rabbit with dried fruit
Served with a lager and tortière seasoned sauce, accompanied by Quinoa with asparagus, carrots, chives and roasted almonds, and a white bean mousseline.

Montérégie duck breast with a sweet and sour chicoutai sauce
Served with baby green beans and chestnuts in brown butter, and potatoes stuffed with sautéed onions, tarragon and goat cheese.

Milk-fed veal filet with morel mushroom sauce from the Québec woodlands Served with mashed celeriac, sautéed artichoke, white turnip, pattypan squash and parsley halloumi.

Quail ballotine stuffed with wild boar and mushrooms, with a blueberry and rosemary sauce
Served with spaghetti squash with shallots and a Portobello blue cheese surprise.
