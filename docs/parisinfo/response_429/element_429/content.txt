                                                            The Pavillon d’Armenonville: an eco-inspired renovation
                                                
                                    



        
            
                
                    
                        
                            The Pavillon d’Armenonville: an eco-inspired renovation
                            After 2 months of work, the Pavillon d’Armenonville reopens its doors, with the greatest respect for the environment. Managed by Butard Enescot, this emblematic place in the 16th arrondissement of Paris is ideal for hosting an eco-event.
                        

                        Once a royal hunting lodge on the edge of the Bois de Boulogne, the Pavilion d’Armenonville has been renovated and modernized with respect for the environment.Modernity is one of its main characteristics with 8 flexible reception rooms bathed in natural light. A total surface area of 2,500 m² means that the Pavilion can host your events, small or large (up to 1,000 people for dinner or a conference and 1,500 for a cocktail reception). This prestigious setting unveils several new features such as the lobby, the glass roof and the terrace of the Etoile reception room, as well as the terrace of the Longchamp reception room.Another main characteristic is the venue’s commitment to the environment with the continuation of the environmental management policy that it has been pursuing for several years, and its desire to obtain the ISO 14001 certification in early 2019.In view of this, Butard Enescot has paid particular attention to the terraces: on the terrace of the Marly reception room, a vegetable garden has been created and a total of 3,000 plants replanted. These are available to the venue’s chefs for them to enhance their dishes with various herbs, vegetables, and even edible flowers. The Group has also carefully selected all materials: Hydro Way® coating for the transformation of paths, the installation of new windows, Led lighting for all areas, etc. And finally, the Pavilion d’Armenonville has just opted for an active policy of sorting, collecting and recycling of waste.
