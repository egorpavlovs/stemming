                                                    Description
                            
                                
        Opened in 1931 by President Paul Doumer, the Maison de la Mutualité has emerged over the years as a unmissable meeting place of the capital. Built in the purest Art Deco style of the 30s and located in the heart of historical Paris between Notre-Dame and the Pantheon, the Maison de la Mutualité is the ideal place to add an extra dimension to all your events. This large-scale project entrusted to the architect Jean-Michel Wilmotte offers you, in Paris, a privileged place which is animated by the idea of cultural sharing which forged its notoriety.
The Maison de la Mutualité hosts more than 300 events of all types in its 3,600 m² of events space at the heart of Paris:
- 1 modular theatre that seats between 180 and 1728 people
- 5 lounges and reception rooms of 100 m² to 800 m²
- 9 lounges for 15 to 120 seats

                                    Accessibility information
                    
        - Physical disability: level or ramped entryway, reception desk with dropped section, adapted bathroom with transfer bar and accessible sink, stairs with alternative means.
- Visual impairment: transparent doors/walls preceded by contrasting signage, no obstacles above head height, stairs with tactile and visual contrasts before first downward step, with continuous handrail, lift with floor announcements and relief and/or Braille buttons, information in large print, signs at a reasonable height. 
- Hearing impairment: lift with clear visual display and/or audible announcements with magnetic hearing loop, flashing light accompanies alarm (where the deaf person may be alone). 
- Intellectual disability: trained staff, easy-to-understand signage with pictograms, monitored entryway visible from cashdesk.

                            Sustainable development approach
                Since 2012, the GL Events network of venues in France is certified ISO 14001, system of environmental management. At the next financial year in January 2018, the network of certified sites will pass a surveillance audit, based on the new version of ISO 14001 version 2015.
