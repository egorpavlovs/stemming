                        Description
                        
                                                    
        Result of 15 years of work and newest Meliá hotel in France, the Meliá Paris La Défense is described by its architects Claude Vasconi and Jean-Philippe Nuel “as an hyphen between the business area’s modernity and the overview it has over Paris”. The hotel aims to guide companies wishing to gather their employees and partners in a privileged environment equipped with state of the art devices. The hotel offers a flexible 9149 square feet event space with a capacity of up to 350 people, as well as 5 meeting rooms located around a 3767 square feet space.
During the length of your seminar, enjoy not only a comfortable 344 square feet room but also, in the majority of the hotel’s 369 rooms and suites, a breathtaking view over the Eiffel Tower and the Louis Vuitton foundation. Then we invite you to seat back and relax while sipping a cocktail at the Skyline Paris Lounge&Bar on the top hotel floor.

                                    Accessibility information
                    
        - Physical disability: car park with adapted reserved spaces, ground-level entrance with access ramp available, adapted reception/welcome desk with till, adapted communal toilets with transfer bar and adapted sink, 9 adapted bedrooms with bathrooms equipped with walk-in showers (with folding and moveable seat), adapted sink, adapted toilet with transfer bar, restaurant with tables higher than 0.7m.
- Visual impairment: transparent doors with warnings in contrasting elements, no obstacles at eye level in the main corridors, stairs with tactile contrast up to 50cm before the first step, continuous handrails, lift with adapted command keys, audio announcement for each floor, information in large font, posters at a reasonable height.
- Hearing impairment: reservation/payment possible on the website, adapted welcome desk with magnetic induction loop, lift with visual announcements, luminous flash with fire alarm (in places where a deaf person may be alone).
- Mental disability: trained staff, easy-to-understand signs, associated pictograms, monitored entrance, visible from reception/till.
