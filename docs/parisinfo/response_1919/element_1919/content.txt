                                                    Description
                            
                                
        Ladurée Royale, the main house since 1862. This coffee shop was established in 1862, is decorated with wood panelling and features original frescoes depicting angelic pastry chefs and voluptuous ladies, and has become a favourite address for people from all walks of life. The menu, renewed with the seasons, offers a simple and tasty cuisine which is inkeeping with Ladurée's reputation. Devotees come to taste the best macaroons in Paris, stuffed croissants and sweets using the great Ladurée traditions.

                                    Accessibility information
                    
        Motor disability: ground floor entrance or entrance with sloping surface, toilet facilities adapted and equipped with transfer bar and sink adapted for wheelchair users, dining area with aisles wider than 1.4 metres with adapted tables (height of 70cm so as not to obstruct wheelchair users).
Visual disability: absence of obstacles at face level to impede circulation, stairs with touch-sensitive and visual contrast before the first descending step, with continuous handrails and contrasting stair edges.
Hearing disability: bookings available to do online.
Mental disability: legible and easy-to-understand signaling system using pictograms, supervised entry and visible from the reception desk.
