                                                    Description
                            
                                
        Located in the heart of the Pavillon Dufour, whose reorganization has been entirely confided to the architects Dominique Perrault and Frédéric Didier, ore is resolutely set in its time. Contemporary reinterpretations of the historical decorative themes evoke the Sun King’s splendor. An exceptional setting and view for this unique venue, open to the public all day. Take a break and enjoy a meal during the palace's opening hours. 
'ore', which means mouth in Latin, is a reference to the pleasures of the mouth anchored in French 'art de vivre' and in particular to Versailles, to the services of the "bouche du Roi", in charge of the meals served to the court.
A contemporary cuisine inspired by history. During the day, a French inspired menu. Classics of our cuisine, alongside light and quick snacks, desserts and gourmet pastries. As always, Alain Ducasse brings the utmost attention to the selection and quality of his produce, giving place to vegetables as a priority. Perfectly mastered preparations, a seasonal, delicious and gourmet menu.
During the evening, an exclusive dining experience. As the palace slowly empties itself, the restaurant becomes a stage for grand dinners and exclusive events. A setting evoking the opulence of days of yore, in the restored dining room. Historical menus, exclusively re-edited Marie-Antoinette porcelain, service « à la française », staff elegantly dressed. A Tailor-made organisation thanks to a vast 500 m2 surface divided into separate spaces: two dining rooms and three private dining salons. Tailor-made organisation for events (cocktail or dinner), from 2 to 120 guests. Partial or complete privatisation.

                                    Accessibility information
                    
        - Physical disability: car park with reserved adapted space(s), collective adapted bathroom with transfer bar and accessible sink, stairs with alternative means (lift, ramp), dining room with aisles larger than 1m40cm to ensure accessibility for wheelchair users.
- Visual impairment: no obstancles above head height, stairs with handrail, lift with floor announcements.
- Hearing impairment: booking and/or payment available online, lift with clear visual display and/or audible announcements with magnetic hearing loop.
- Intellectual disability: easy-to-understand signage with pictograms, monitored entryway visible from cashdesk.
