                            Urbanism
                            All of the city's development programs: transportation projects, train stations and airports ...
                        

                        The city's development programsOngoing projectsRedesigning of Madeleine square: creation of a car-free area and landscaping (until summer 2019)   Redesign of Place de la Bastille into a large walking area (until 2020)  Transformation of the Morland Tower in the 4th arrondissement into a 5-star hotel with pool, a youth hostel, a food market, a bar, a panoramic restaurant and an Art Lab (until 2021)  Renovation and extension of Paris Expo Porte de Versailles: 7 halls (216,000 m²) entirely redesigned and renovated, creation of a 5,200-seat convention centre and 97 meeting rooms, creation of 400 hotel rooms (2-3*), a gourmet restaurant by Guy Martin, a 2,600-m² shopping mall, gardens, plant roof and tennis courts. Associated architects: Jean Nouvel, Christian de Portzamparc and Dominique Perrault (until 2025)Upcoming projectsOpening of the "Moulin Seine", floating bakery moored at Port des Invalides by the Alexandre-III bridge (2019)  Redesign of Place de la Nation with new lanes, bicycle lanes and pedestrian areas (2019)  Creation of a 7,000-square-meter garden at Porte de Versailles, off Boulevard Victor (summer 2019)  Opening of a green walkway between Buttes Chaumont and La Villette parks (summer 2019)  Creation in Pantin of a marina on the Ourcq Canal (2019)  Opening on Ialie Avnue of Italik, mall with 3 restaurants, 9 shops, space for special events and leisure (fin 2019)  Full renovation of Montparnasse Tower: replacement of the glass facade and 18-meter rooftop extension with a green house (late 2019 - mid-2023)  Launch of the "Grand site Tour Eiffel" project, including full renovation of the access and visit experience at the Tower (2019-2024)  Inauguration of 6 fountains at Rond-point des Champs-Élysées with a new design by Ronan et Erwan Bouroullec (2020)  In the 13th arrondissement, construction by Jean Nouvel of Duo, an ensemble of two towers (180 and 122 metres) with auditorium, 1,832 m² of shops, hotel 4* with 120 bedrooms and panoramic restaurant with terrace (2020)  Construction of a fashion and arts craftsmanship manufactory by Chanel at Porte d'Aubervilliers (2020)  Creation of a 3-hectare hanging garden by Jean Nouvel at La Défense on the Rose de Cherbourg Viaduc, and building the Hekla 220-metre tower (2020)  Inauguration at La Défense of the two 323-m high Hermitage towers, with function room, auditorium, contemporary art gallery, shops, hotel 4* with 210 rooms, conference centre and restaurants (2020)  Creation in Tremblay en France, close to Paris – Charles-de-Gaulle airport, of the Aérolians project, a 200-hectare complex for trade between Europe, China and South-East Asia, specializing in renewable energy, with two hotels 4* and 800 bedrooms, wine museum and congress centre (2020)  Conversion of a former 1908 power station into a 5-theatre cinema and training restaurant by chef Thierry Marx in the 11th arrondissement (mid-2020)  Building of the UChicago Center in Paris campus in the 13th arrondissement, designed by the architect Jeanne Gang (mid-2020 - 2022)  Construction at Porte de Versailles of the 180-m high Triangle tower with panoramic lift and viewpoint, gourmet restaurant, 4* hotel with 120 rooms, garden, conference center and cultural space ; a project by Swiss architects Herzog & de Meuron (2020-2024)  Creation of "Colline de Gibraltar", immense park and hill in Champs sur Marne (2020-2032)  Inauguration at La Défense of the Sisters towers, project of 200- and 100-meter high towers by French architect Christian de Portzamparc, with a 80-meter high walkway with garden, spa, screening room and restaurant. One of the towers will be a 4-star 300-room hotel (2021)  Redesigning of the gardens and the entrance of the Eiffel Tower (2021-2024)  Opening of a congress centre at Roissy, the International Trade Center Paris, with 120 modular meeting rooms, 3,000-seater auditorium, 4 700-sq.mt. plenary rooms, three exhibition halls totaling 41,000 m², 1,840 hotel rooms in 7 hotels (3-5*), 22,000 m² of office space, 8 restaurants and services (early 2022)  Reformation of the Pleyel Tower in Saint-Denis with 2 hotels / 700 rooms in 3* & 4*, pool and panoramic lounge on the roof, conference center and restaurants (2022) Redevelopment of the Grand Palais, Galeries nationales and the Palais de la découverte sites (until 2023). Building of an ephemeral Grand Palais Champ de Mars from 2020 to 2023.  Redesign of Porte Maillot: public transport, urbanism, circulation (2023)  Redesign of 50,000 sq. mt. on the Île Seguin with a contemporary art foundation by RCR Arquitectes, a 220-room hotel by Baumschlager Eberle architects, a 15,000 sq. mt. garden, shops and office space (until 2023) Renovation of the open-air museum in La Défense (2023)  Renovation and extension of Hall 3 at Le Bourget (2023)  Opening of Arena 2, 7,500-seat multi-sport arena at Porte de la Chapelle, dedicated to basket and handball (2024)  Creation of the Chapelle-Charbon park to the North of Paris between Porte de la Chapelle and Porte d'Aubervilliers. 6.5 hectares opening in phases (2020-2030) Modernization work on the Stade de France and construction of a nearby congress centre with 1,500 places and 500 bedrooms (date to be defined) Creation of a 2,000-m² garden at 82 Avenue Denfert-Rochereau in the 14th arrondissement (date to be defined)  Creation of a leisure area in Médan to the West of Paris along the Seine river and on the Platais island: renovation of a 1935 Art Deco building, creation of a 50-room 4* hotel, a spa, a gourmet restaurant, a guinguette and a Dreyfus museum in the former house of Émile Zola (date to be defined) The city's transportation projects, train stations and airportsRenovation of Paris – Orly airport with the combining of the south and west terminals into a single large terminal adapted to large carriers like the A380 (until 2019)  Creation of a bicycle lane on Champs-Élysées avenue (until December 2019)  Renovation of Montparnasse train station: creation of waiting rooms and 130 shops on 19,000 sq. mt. (until November 2020)  Renovation of the Austerlitz train station by Jean Nouvel with creation of a glass roof, hotels and 20,000 m² of shopping area all connected with a forecourt and a monument stair case (until 2021)  Closing of line 6 of the Métro between Montparnasse and Trocadéro (mid-July to late August 2019 and again in 2020)   Extension of metro line 12 over 2 stations to the north of Paris as far as d’Aubervilliers town hall (late 2019)  Renovation and extension of the Gare du Nord with creation of a new departures terminal, a business and coworking centre, a duty free shopping area, a theatre and a fitness and trail area on the roof (late 2019 - 2024)  Extension of metro line 14 to the au north from Saint-Lazare to Mairie de Saint-Ouen, through Porte de Clichy (summer 2020) and to the south as far as Paris – Orly airport (2024) Opening of a high-speed train line between the Paris – Charles-de-Gaulle airport and Amiens via Chantilly (2020) 6-km extension of metro line 11to the east of Paris, and creation of 6 new stations from Montreuil to Rosny-Bois-Perrier (2020) Extension of "Eole", line E of the RER, over 55 km West of Paris from Saint-Lazare to Porte Maillot, La Défense, Nanterre (2020) and beyond as far as Mantes-la-Jolie (2022)  Automation work and extension of metro line 4 to the south of Paris towards Bagneux (2022) Extension of the tramway to the north-west of Paris, from Porte d’Asnières to Porte Dauphine (2023)  Opening of a 20-minute direct train line between Paris – Charles-de-Gaulle airport and Gare de l’Est in Paris, CDG Express (2024)  Opening of an aerial tram between the train stations Gare de Lyon and Gare d'Austerlitz (2024)  Opening of a high-speed train line Paris - Toulouse in 3:10 hours (2024) Creation of a 4th terminal at Paris – Charles-de-Gaulle airport (2024-2037)  Creation of a TGV train station at Paris – Orly airport (2025) Extension to the east of Paris of Métro line 1, to Montreuil and as far as Val de Fontenay (2030) Extension to the south of Paris of Métro line 10as far as Ivry sur Seine (2030) Development of anexpress metro network around Paris, with 200 km of lines and 72 stations (2022-2030)  Launch of Téléval, urban aerial tram connecting Créteil, Limeil-Brévannes, Valenton and Villeneuve Saint-Georges in the east of Paris (date to be announced)  Inauguration of an electric tram-bus line along the Seine river on the upper right bank (date to be announced)
