                            Behind the scenes at private events in Paris with Muriel Chapuis
                            Paris is full of exceptional places that every year play host to some of the most beautiful private evening functions in the world. The editor of the popular online magazine News Evénement(s), Muriel Chapuis, shares her favourite events of 2017 with us and analyses future trends for professionals in the sector.
                        

                        With its monuments and powerful romantic symbolism rooted in the collective unconscious, the French capital is a natural and inspiring location for many international films, luxury advertisements and all kinds of clips. Every year, it also welcomes huge sporting, cultural and promotional events thanks to unusual places and organizers who are delighted by all these possibilities. Muriel Chapuis is a teacher in event communications and editorial director of the magazine News Evénement(s).  She decodes major past and future trends in venues and types of events for us.In your opinion, what were the most impressive events of the year in Paris? Muriel Chapuis: It was impossible not to be aware of the temporary infrastructures created by the agency Ubi Bene as part of Paris’s bid to host the 2024 Olympic Games. In particular, the 156-metre-long floating athletics track on the Seine, between the Alexandre III Bridge and the Invalides Bridge! The same agency came up with the idea of big ‘aviaries’ on the Champs-Elysées, with a track and special events for the Paris Drone Festival. Another memorable event was the Athem & Skertzo spectacle projected onto the Louvre pyramid to celebrate the inauguration of the Louvre Abu Dhabi, and also the lighting scenography by Tetro for the inauguration of theParis Convention Centre, which included light effects stretching across the Paris ring road. There was also BiodiversiTerre, with plant scenography by Gad Weil and his agency Place Grand Public, on Avenue Foch …10,000 m² to raise the awareness of Parisians and tourists about environmental issues.Brands are also vying with one another in their ingenuity aren’t they?Muriel Chapuis: Yes indeed, the Saint Laurent fashion show during Fashion Week was a memorable event. There was an amazing catwalk set up in the Trocadéro gardens across from the Eiffel Tower, which began to sparkle. All orchestrated by the agency Betak and with the assistance of Jaulin for the structure. There was also the funfair-inspired big wheel, installed by Seat and its agency Double 2 at La Villette, on the occasion of the launch of the new Ibiza. The vehicles were placed in the pods, and it was possible to have a ride on the wheel! Then there was Cartier’s poetic and olfactory installation for the Fiac, on the square at the Palais de Tokyo. Named the perfumed cloud OSNI.1, this work in the form of a glass and metal cube offered the public the opportunity to walk through a fragrant floating mist. Or the Heetch event, designed by the Manifestory agency, with a physical component, which was a street demonstration, and a digital component.What do Parisian event organizers have in store for us in 2018?Muriel Chapuis: It is difficult to know what the big events for 2018 will be apart from the recurring cultural and sporting events or trade fairs and congress planned in advance. All so-called private corporate events, such as product launches, company anniversary celebrations, marketing events …are rarely known in advance, if at all. But as we can see from 2017, street events are always very successful, due to their often spectacular nature, their original use of the public space and their ambition to appear to the greatest number of people. Events that bring people together and use digital technology, named ‘phygital’ are also very popular and will certainly develop.What in particular will appeal to the generation of millennials, French people born between 1980 and 1995 or 2000?Muriel Chapuis: What generally appeals to millennials are events that give rise to meeting and talking to others, the link within a community. Also, events that transmit a sense and authenticity, and that come across as ‘sincere, true and ethical’ … Events that are live, digital and technology-based, and which this generation cannot do without.
