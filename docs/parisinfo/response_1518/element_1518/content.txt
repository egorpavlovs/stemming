                                                    Description
                            
                                
        The art deco architecture of the Atrium Saint-Germain, built at the end of the 19th-century, is distinguished by its amazing 15-metre-high glass roof. The spaces were completely renovated in 2017 to provide a high-quality offering. Ideally located in the heart of the 7th arrondissement of Paris, the spaces of the Atrium Saint-Germain can accommodate up to 200 people over some 500 m². They consist of the following 9 rooms: the Atrium (100 m²) with its reception on the ground floor; the Maubert room (100 m²) which can be opened along its length with the Atrium, via a removable partition, to double its surface area; the Récamier room (21 m²) adjacent to the Atrium; the Mabillon room (21 m²) adjacent to the Atrium; the Montparnasse room (120 m²) on the lower ground floor; the Pasteur room (50 m²) communicating with the Montparnasse room via removable partitions to give a space of 170 m²; the Babylone room (35 m²) communicating with the Montparnasse room via removable partitions, providing a space of 200 m²; the Opera room (20 m²)  on the 1st floor of the building can host up to 20 people; the Montmartre room (30 m²) on the 3rd floor of the building can host up to 20 people.

                                    Accessibility information
                    
        - Motor disability: car park with reserved adapted spaces, ground level entrance or inclined surface, adapted reception/checkout, common area toilet equipped with a transfer bar and adapted washbasin.
-Visual impairment: transparent doors/windows walls indicated by contrasting elements, absence of obstacles at face level in areas of circulation, staircase with tactile and visual contrasts before the first step down with continuous handrail, with contrasting edges of each step, lift with adapted controls (raised buttons and/or in braille), with sound-proofed cabin with floors announced, information in large letters, displayed at a reasonable height.
-Hearing impairment: reception/checkout equipped with a magnetic induction loop.
-Mental disability: trained/informed staff, simple to understand signage, easy to read, including pictograms.
