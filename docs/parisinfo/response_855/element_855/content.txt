                                                    Description
                            
                                
        Go behind the scenes of luxury French leather goods, FÃ©oni opens the doors of his workshop and shares his know-how with lovers of beautiful factories.
The FÃ©oni workshop offers an introduction to leather goods that combines the personalization of a bag and the discovery of a craft. A day to make a bag, made-to-measure, accompanied by Baptiste designer-leatherworker and his team.
In order to optimize our clients' bags, the FÃ©oni workshop provides the necessary raw materials and professional equipment: a professional workbench and leather goods tools. We take care of finishing and rendering as for the manufacturing standards.
Each workshop takes place in a small committee (8 pers max) to facilitate the exchanges and to help each "apprentice" to express his tastes and to mount his article with confidence. Our workshops are designed to guarantee a satisfying result to our customers.
How do we operate?
We offer the possibility to our customers to make a bag or a portfolio in semi-measurement. From a model, the customer will be able to choose different options: the materials, the interior storage, the type of closures, the design of the handles (for the bag) and the finishes.
These choices can be made directly on our website or by appointment at our workshop.
We then book a workshop date together. We open a workshop for three people. We offer the possibility to privatize a workshop.
Making a bag takes a day from 9:30 AM to 5:30 PM, including a lunch break.
The portfolio workshop takes place over a half-day, in the morning from 9:00 AM to 12:00 or in the afternoon from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM.
The time may vary depending on the number of options chosen.
Our initiation workshops on leather goods have been designed for the general public and do not require any prerequisites.
Our subjects
We use leather of high qualities of cattle and sheep, French or Italian tanneries. Our leathers come from our customers and other luxury homes that sell their end of series.
We also use, depending on demand, exotic leathers or alternatives to leather such as cork or pineapple textile.
Our jewelers come from reputable suppliers holding the EPV (Living Heritage Company) label, with whom we use to work.
Activity Details
What our workshops include: everything needed to make the bag: materials (leather, jewelry), leatherworking tools, access to machinery, a professional workbench, an apron
Possibility to organize lunch on site optional.
Level: without pre-requisites
Activity: children over 12 years accompanied by an adult and adults
Languages: fluent English, Japanese concept. Possibility to animate a course with a translator.
