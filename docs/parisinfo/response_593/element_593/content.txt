                                    Accessibility information
                    
        -Physical disability: the Grande Galerie de l'Evolution and the Galerie des Enfants are accessible to visitors in wheelchairs with adapted toilets. The height of furniture and machines has been well thought out. No access to the Galerie de Pal√©ontologie and Galerie d'Anatomie Compar√©e. Some others sites in the museum are partially accessible with an accompanying person (M√©nagerie du Jardin des Plantes). 
-All disabilities: permanent exhibitions are free (except for tactile visits), guided tours, workshops with speakers. Guided tours and workshops are specially designed using pedagogical materials to suit the different publics. Audio guides and adapted routes can be downloaded at http://www.museesenliberte.fr/index.php
-Visual impairment: in the Grande Galerie de l'Evolution, a tactile tour enables the visitor to discover some 15 animal sculptures in bronze. Descriptions are in contrasting colours, large characters and relief are used. 
-Audio impairment: guided tours and workshops in French Sign Language, lead by deaf speakers. All multimedia and video material, including in the language spoken, are subtitled in French and English and translated into French Sign Language.
