                                                    Description
                            
                                
        With its 55 hectares, spectacular architecture, and 10 million annual visitors, the largest park in Paris offers a unique spectrum of spaces for your promotional activities, teambuilding, product launches, shoots, temporary structures, recreational activities, big events...
La Villette houses an exceptional range of places allowing multiple configurations. Each space has its own identity and offers an infinite range of activities.
Small selection of some spaces:
The Prairie du Triangle: located at the park entrance, between the Great Hall and the Philharmonie, the Prairie du Triangle is the largest lawn in Paris at 20,400 m².
La Prairie du Cercle: this space allows a unique viewpoint on the canal and the Géode. La Prairie du Cercle is divided in two by the passage of the canal forming two spaces: the Northern Circle Prairie (9,900 m²) and the South Circle Prairie (12,600 m²).
The Place de la Fontaine aux Lions: a real gateway to the site, this 14,700 m² square gives access to all the park's sites and activities. An area of 2,400 m² can be available for your installation.
    
                            
                        
                                                Viewpoint
                        
        Themed gardens:
The garden walk, or cinematic walk, unravels like a film reel tossed haphazadly over the park.
The  walker discovers, according to successive framing, a succession of twelve thematic gardens separated by footbridges and, at night, by a luminous dotted line. Depending on theirmood, the walker can explore different garden configurations: playgrounds, tree-planted grounds, sound elements, aquatic atmospheres and intimate spaces conducive to reflection.
Passenger Gardens
Created in 2001, the passenger gardens were inspired by the exhibition "The Planetary Garden", presented in 1999 in the Great Hall by the landscape gardener Gilles Clément, whose intention was to raise public awareness of major environmental issues. At the site of the former sheep market, this ecological island of 3000m² houses various ecosystems: grove, wasteland, pond, and dry stone wall. Ecological gardens with an educational and solidarity role, the passenger gardens serve as support for educational workshops to raise awareness of biodiversity for young and old. This state of mind encourages the experimentation of organic farming methods and a closer observation of the living.

                                    Accessibility information
                    
        Street level access from the Porte de la Villette or by the Porte de Pantin. Accessible toilets (often without grab rail) in all areas of the park. The Parc de la Villette is completely accessible, but the aid of an extra person is needed in some spots. +33 (0) 1 40 03 75 75.
There is a car park reserved for visitors with reduced mobility.
