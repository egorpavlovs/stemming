                            Guides, brochures and maps
                            Discover our Paris city guides and publications for professionals! Full of practical information for organizing your stay and event in Paris. You can view the guides and brochures as an e-zine or request for brochures.
                        

                        City guidesParis city map-guide 2018-2019The Paris city map-guide is the ideal resource for exploring Paris and its region. You will find a detailed map of the capital and its immediate surroundings, a public transport map and six pages of practical information. The map-guide is available at all the Visitors Bureau’s information points in 10 languages (English, French, German, Italian, Spanish, Portuguese, Russian, Chinese, Japanese and Korean).> Request printed guides in English> Request printed guides in FrenchParis City Guide 2018-2019This pocket guide helps you to explore Paris and its surroundings. You plan your stay around not-to-be-missed key sites, near to which are suggested museums, monuments, parks and gardens, and districts … to discover. The French capital is carved up into 7 major districts, including addresses in the Hauts-de-Seine, Seine-Saint-Denis and Val-de-Marne, as well of course a chapter on the Île-de-France. The guide includes a themed section packed with ideas and  addresses on the most in-demand topics from visitors. You will find information about trips & tours, gastronomy, shopping, family outings, and nightlife. Not forgetting a practical information section with useful tips for your stay.> View the guide as an e-zine> Request printed guides in English> Request printed guides in FrenchParis Guided Tours and Visits 2018-2019This bilingual booklet lists interpreter-guides, lecturer-guides and companies specializing in cultural or thematic tours of Paris. On foot, by boat, by sightseeing coach, by bicycle, by air-balloon ... not forgetting ideas for discovering gastronomy, oenology, shopping, behind-the-scenes, etc.> View the guide as an e-zineAccessible Paris 2019-2020This  guide is published in French and in English. It makes planning a trip to Paris easier for people with disabilities (physical, visual, hearing and mental). It is packed with all the essential information on how to get to Paris - by plane, train or car-, how to get around on public transport or by private transport, restaurants and accommodation for groups, visits to museums and monuments, and places to go and outings (theatres, shopping, theme parks). A sightseeing itinerary adapted to independent wheelchair users along the Seine riverbanks is featured at the end of the guide (Pages 26 and 27).> View the guide as en ez-ine in English> View the guide as an e-zine in FrenchPublications for professionalsWhat's up in Paris 2019What's up in Paris is an essential magazine to give you extra help in planning your events and trips in Parisl. Luxury and boutique hotels, as well as accommodation for young people, unusual venues, top restaurants, incentives, nightlife, shopping … What’s Up in Paris introduces you to ‘the’ places to be in the capital from the hottest to the unmissable.> Discover the magazine> Get a copyParis Committee Sales Manual 2019The Paris Committee Sales Manual 2019  is a professional publication produced by the Leisure Section of the Marketing Department and aimed especially at the Paris Committee markets (South East Asia, mainland China, South Korea, Colombia and Argentina, Russia with Belarus and Ukraine). It lists 124 sites in Paris and the region positioned on the Leisure and/or Business segment according to 4 categories: Leisure (places to visit), Tours & Transport, Shopping and Accommodation. It is an essential tool for all foreign tour operators looking for new products and business contacts to initiate negotiations or develop existing relations.Download the Paris Committee Sales Manual 2019Meeting in Paris 2018-2019The Meeting in Paris guide is the essential tool for event planners. Designed for agencies, companies and associations, it features some 700 Parisian service providers with key information and photos. These service providers are listed under major categories: conference/exhibition centres, accommodation, event venues, PCOs & DMCs, incentive activities, and other services. This biennial bilingual French-English directory also presents the attractions of the city in key figures, a ‘did you know?’ and infographics, as well as a calendar of upcoming and current events in Paris and indispensable media and services for the planning of an event.> Browse the guide> Request download
