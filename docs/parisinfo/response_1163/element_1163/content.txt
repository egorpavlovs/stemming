                                                            Interview Alain Pichavant, Nautic Paris International Boat Show
                                                
                                    



        
            
                
                    
                        
                            Interview Alain Pichavant, Nautic Paris International Boat Show
                            Alain Pichavant, Commissioner General of the Nautic Paris International Boat Show, relates the history of the show for us and briefs us on changes and what’s new at this Parisian event. The 58th edition of the Nautic Paris International Boat Show will be held from 8 to 16 December at Paris Expo Porte de Versailles!

                        

                        In a few words, how would you sum up the Paris Boat Show?The Nautic Paris International Boat Show originated in 1962. This year will be its 58th edition. It is a show that has changed enormously; it began when boating appeared in France and has followed all the trends in the industry. It is now the 5th biggest French trade show. A few statistics: 36% of visitors are Parisians, 56% are from the provinces. The share of international visitors is 8%.What have been the highlights of this show since it was created?In 1962, for its first edition, the boating industry was quite low key. The changes in the show reflect the changes in the industry:In 1964, after Eric Tabarly won the solo Trans-Atlantic Race - Plymouth-Newport, the French discovered boating. This victory marked the history of offshore racing as well as the start of a passion on the part of the French for the pleasures of boating.Sales of sail boats and motorized boats have continued to develop into the 21st century. The oil crisis of the 1970s was just a blip in this dynamic market. The boating industry continued to do well.In 1987, the show left the CNIT Paris La Défense for the Porte de Versailles which had a larger surface area.From 1987 to 2008, the sector continued to grow.In 2008, following the subprime economic crisis, the global boating industry was strongly impacted. It only began to recover in 2011. During this period, the show had fewer exhibitors and a reduced exhibition area.Since 2012 and economic recovery, the French boating industry has been doing better; our country exports three-quarters of its production – a record for a sector of industrial activity. In addition to this upturn in the industry, the Nautic has resumed its dynamism. The show has been growing since 2016. It has changed its orientation to adapt to the new consumption patterns of younger people which involve the notions of sharing, experiences, and multiple activities.What is on the programme for this year?On 9 December, Nautic paddle – the biggest paddle race in the world – will be held.  This year will be the 9th edition and 800 competitors will be taking part! This is a unique and atypical race because it passes under 27 of the 29 bridges in Paris during the month of December! In total, 29 nationalities are represented and 35% of participants are women.We are also organizing the 3rd edition of Nautic Street Fishing. It is a fishing competition in which fish are fished but not killed, and instead put back into the water; in teams of 2, on the Seine riverbanks.A big first: Nautic Hackathon, a 24-hour non-stop event that will take place on 14 and 15 December! The teams, accompanied by specialist consultants, have to reflect on their vision of what a water sports centre will be between now and 2030.How strong is the nautical sector?In France, the nautical sector is buoyant. It has returned to its 2008 pre-crisis level. This positive situation is due in part to exports, technology and national expertise, but also due both to boating activities and to all water sports activities such as surfing, stand-up paddle, dingy sailing, canoeing, kayaking, wakeboarding, and water skiing … The sheer pleasure of being on the water!By way of an example, every year, 90,000 new boat licenses are passed – a figure that somewhat reflects the passion of the French for water sports!Why choose Paris for the organization of this show? Paris has a large population with strong purchasing power. The French capital has a lot of magnetism! And the Nautic is a fine testimony to a sector whose image and dynamism are one of the models of French industry.
