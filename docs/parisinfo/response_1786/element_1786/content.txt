                                                    Description
                            
                                
        The Musée de la Vie Romantique, located on rue Chaptal, is dedicated to the arts of the Bourbon Restoration and the July Monarchy, especially writer George Sand and painter Ary Scheffer.
Scheffer lived in the house for nearly thirty years, painted a lot of his works there and received the artistic elite of his time in the workshop and lounge: Chopin, Liszt, Lamartine, Delacroix, Rossini, Thiers and Dickens.
The collection consists of a number of paintings, other pieces of art and furniture which illustrate this brilliant period.
Host your event in the museum's garden, a charming green setting rare for Paris, in the heart of the 'Nouvelle Athènes' area.

                                    Accessibility information
                    
        The garden and the workshops are accessible. The central pavilion is not accessible. 
For visitors with sight impairments: touch tours with a talk.
For visitors with mental disabilities: tours and stories based on the collections and the exhibitions.
