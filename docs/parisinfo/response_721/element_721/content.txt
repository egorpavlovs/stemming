                        Description
                        
                                                    
        The Mercure Paris CDG Airport & Convention welcomes you to the heart of the airport and is ideal for those travelling to Paris on business. Enjoy an uninterrupted view of the runways from your room or meeting room, and there are many facilities at your disposal to help you enjoy your stay, such as free Wi-Fi, pool terrace, gym, gaming, entertainment, lounge bar and 24 hour catering.
The hotel offers 18 meeting rooms and business lounges across 2 floors, and welcomes all kinds of events: conventions, training sessions, workshops, executive meetings, conferences, cocktail parties and more.
Our business spaces can be adapted to suit any event, becoming a lecture theatre, classroom or banquet hall. Each rooms includes notepads, pens, paperboards, water and all your required projection equipment.
The Mercure Paris CDG Airport & Convention puts your comfort first, with 345 rooms and suites, all equipped with high-quality bedding and fittings, including tripe glazing for soundproofing, Wi-Fi and individually-controlled air-conditioning.
The hotel offers various fun and surprising teambuilding exercises such as puzzles, Kapla and colouring to help your colleagues work together. Or how about a cooking competition, casino night, or a yoga or salsa class? Whatever you choose, you're sure to have an unforgettable stay bonding with your colleagues.
Our team are experts in professional event management, and offering you a warm and unique welcome is our top priority.

                            Sustainable development approach
                Since 2005, the Environmental Charter of the Hotelier recommends 65 actions to be implemented by the establishments to effectively reduce their impact on the environment (glass recycling, recovery of rainwater, use of ecolabelled products, etc.).
Each year, the hoteliers indicate the actions of the Environmental Charter that are implemented, so as to evaluate their performance compared to the previous year and in relation to their area. In addition to a reporting tool, the Environmental Charter is a management tool.
This Charter was updated in 2011 and renamed as part of the Group's new PLANET21 strategy. The indicators have been updated and extended to societal actions such as the integration of fair trade products, the organization of training for health and well-being, etc .; keeping 65 checkpoints.
Charter 21 is now structured in 5 parts: management, energy, water, waste, products.
Charter 21 gives hotels a framework of actions for their sustainable development approach, helping them to progress and monitor their performance over time. To encourage hotels to rely on the Charter 21, AccorHotels has developed a system of Bronze, Silver, Gold and Platinum levels to accompany hotels in the gradual deployment of actions first basic and then more and more thorough. The level system is cumulative: the hotel must first validate one level before being able to access the next one.
This approach of support and continuous improvement shows today convincing results in terms of dissemination of good practices since today 89% of the hotels have at least the Bronze level. Since 2013, the reality of the implementation of the first ten actions of the Charter 21 to reach the Bronze level is audited by an external firm as part of the quality audits.
