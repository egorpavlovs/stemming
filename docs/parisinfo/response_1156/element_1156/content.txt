                            EuroPCR Congress 2018: why one of the biggest meetings of cardiologists has chosen to come to Paris again?
                            From 22 to 25 May, the Palais des Congrès is hosting the EuroPCR Course, the official annual meeting of the European Association of Percutaneous Cardiovascular Interventions (EAPCI), organized by Europa Organisation. Jean Fajadet, a cardiologist from Toulouse and co-director of the EuroPCR Course, explains why the Palais des Congrès perfectly meets the event’s needs.

                        

                        Why has the city of Paris been chosen to host the 2018 EuroPCR congress?Jean Fajadet: The first EuroPCR congress started in Toulouse in 1989, in order to share information on new discoveries in interventional cardiology. This consisted of complementary cardiological examinations or endovascular treatments, i.e. introducing catheters and probes into arteries and veins. Over the years, the event has grown, reaching 4,000 attendees in the late 1990s. That’s when the event moved to the magnificent Palais des Congrès de la Porte-Maillot. Today, we welcome 11,000 participants, half of whom come from outside Europe: Asia, the Middle East, North America … Thanks to its airport links with the world’s capitals, Paris has the advantage of being easily accessible for these visitors. Additionally, there are a large number of hotels easily accessible from Porte Maillot.As a medical professional, what are your expectations for the congress?Jean Fajadet: As part of the EuroPCR congress, we need to stream live demonstrations of percutaneous cardiovascular interventions to different countries, such as France, Germany, or even China. These therapeutic acts are streamed in real time to several rooms in the Palais des Congrès. The participants therefore attend the entire demonstration, step by step. At any moment, they can ask the speakers questions via an interactive tablet. These live transmissions enable us to address the two aims of the EuroPCR Course: on the one hand to teach new techniques to cardiologists, and on the other hand to show them the latest innovations, new tools, etc. How do you use the different spaces in the Palais des Congrès?Jean Fajadet: The satellite transmissions are shown in the Main Arena, which can seat up to 3,700 people, as well as in some adjacent rooms, which each have a capacity for around 1,000 people. In total, we use some fifteen rooms at any one time, which could be for viewing demonstrations, attending more traditional presentations, or discussions with other specialists. With experience, we have learned to optimize the spaces and the sessions. We have become the world’s leading meeting for interventional cardiology. The smooth running of the course and the screenings are therefore a real technical challenge. This requires a great deal of professionalism from the teams at the Palais des Congrès, and, so far, we have always been fully satisfied with the welcome and services provided.
