                        Description
                        
                                                    
        Strategically located at Porte Maillot facing the Paris Convention Center, a few minutes from the Champs-Elysées and La Défense business district, the hotel offers one of the largest collections of rooms (1025) and meeting areas in Europe, in a contemporary and modern style.
Through creative and original programs, Le Méridien surprises clients and inspires them to come up with new ideas, in a dynamic and stimulating environment.
The 2,500 m² conference centre, including 25 totally-renovated modular rooms, provides a made-to-measure welcome for your events with up
to 1,200 guests, including: business breakfasts, product launches, conferences, seminars, study days and gala dinners. Discover our daily delegate packages
for inspired meetings.
Our restaurants and bars offer you a taste of creative cuisine with innovative flavours: 
Ma Chère & Tendre steakhouse restaurant: enjoy the best cuts of meat combined with a selection of 30 wines by the glass.
The Jazz Club Etoile: one of the most legendary jazz clubs in Paris welcomes you to a chic and intimate setting for live concerts on Fridays and Saturdays.
Bar and restaurant open every day.
Stay in shape with the fitness centre open 24/7.

        Sustainable 
    
    
        

                            Sustainable development approach
                A Clef Verte award holder since 2005, and entry on the "Pour un hébergement durable à Paris" charter by the OTCP since 2012, the Méridien Etoile has done all it can to improve their management with respect to the environment.
Repsonsable meetings: The Méridien Etoile is commited to supporting the efforts of its clients in the organisation of more environmentally friendly meetings by offering green solutions to reduce the environmental impact of their event packages. Eco-Reponsible Study Days are offered at no additional cost, more electronic communication, breaks and lunches using local, in-season produce, and a study on the issues of transport and energy. 
Local, in-season cuisine: the chef is committed to offering local cuisine and seasonal recipes with ingredients from less than 200 km away from Paris.
