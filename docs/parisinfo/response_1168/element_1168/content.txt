                                                            Interview: 5 questions to Philippe Brocart, Managing Director of the Maison&Objet trade show
                                                
                                    



        
            
                
                    
                        
                            Interview: 5 questions to Philippe Brocart, Managing Director of the Maison&Objet trade show
                            In this interview, Philippe Brocart traces the history of Maison&Objet, a biennial trade show dedicated to home decor and interior design. The opportunity also to learn more about the trends and features of this new edition which will be held from 18 to 22 January 2019 at the Parc des Expositions de Paris Nord Villepinte.
                        

                        In a few words, how would you describe the Maison & Objet trade show? The concept of the Maison&Objet trade show is unique in the world due to its transversal nature: twice per year, it gathers all of the players in the sectors of decoration, the art of living and design. In addition to what its exhibitors have to offer, Maison&Objet creates events, forums and conferences that contribute to inspiring trend setters and buyers from around the worldWhat are the objects and furniture design trends this year?Trends are first and foremost linked to trends in society and Maison&Objet seeks to decrypt them notably through its Trend Forum, its ‘Book of Inspirations’ Trend Mook, its area devoted to what’s new or through talks that are held during the show. Products and designs that meet environmental, social or ethical issues for example are in tune with the times. Consumers are indeed looking for authenticity, unique pieces or limited series; they are interested in the origins of products (who designed it, who made it, where was it made?...)‘Bye Bye routine, hello surprise. We evolve for you’, sets the tone for this new edition. Can you tell us more? In September 2018, we reorganized the show into two major branches: ‘House’ organized by style and ‘Object’ organized by product categories. This makes it easier for buyers to find their way around and is more logical. The slogan was the opportunity to show that reorganizing the show was a means of giving people an opportunity to discover new brands and new products because discovering new products is the major motivation for visiting a trade show.What makes Maison&Objet different from other shows devoted to home decor and design?Its transversal nature, its desire to decrypt but also its strong digital presence both on social media (more than 532,000 Instagram subscribers) and thanks to its digital platform MOM that makes it possible to discover the latest products from brands throughout the year.Why choose Paris for the show?     Paris is a city of reference for design, decoration and the art of living. French designers, interior designers and decorators are much sought after and work throughout the world. It is therefore logical that Maison&Objet takes place amidst this abundance of creativity that is a permanent source of inspiration for our community.
