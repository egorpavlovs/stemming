                                                    Description
                            
                                
        The Poulbot reception room is destined for its purpose. Located on the ground floor of the hotel, it can welcome up to 50 people for dinner or 100 for a cocktail party and any other type of event (seminar, dinner, cocktails, conferences, breakfast..). The room is available for hire all year round. On the outskirts of Renoir Gardens, it can be opened onto our outdoor spaces and thus increase the capacity (the gardens are only available for evening hire).
The private rooms are museum rooms. Only two rooms can host a meal (sitting or standing); the others are made available for the presented visits (visit or speaker). The three Renoir gardens are included in the privatisation of space and have a higher capacity than that of the museum (150 - 200 people). Times for hire: daily from 8am-10am (breakfast reception), 6:30pm-8:30pm (appetizer visit) or 6:30pm-11:30pm (cocktails or dinner).
    
                            
                        
                                                Viewpoint
                        
        The Renoir Gardens have an exceptional view of the Clos Montmartre vineyard and beyond there, the north of Paris.

                                                Garden
                        
        The Renoir Gardens were reconstructed in memory of Auguste Renoir, an impressionist painter, who lived here from 1876 to 1877. During his stay he painted some of his most famous works such as le Bal du Moulin de la Galette ('Dance at Le Moulin de la Galette') and La Balançoire ('The Swing').

                                    Accessibility information

- Visual impairment: No obstales above shoulder height in the corridors, stairs with handrail and contrasting edges.
- Intellectual disability: monitored entryway, visible from welcome desk
