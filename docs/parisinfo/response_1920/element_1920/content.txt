                                                    Description
                            
                                
        This establishment, located at 75 Avenue des Champs-Elysées, opened in September 1997 and recreates the sophisticated atmosphere of the rue Royale in a unique place which is a symbol of the French way of life. On the ground floor, the Eiffel glass wall and the terrace offer a great view of the Champs-Elysées. The bar, which is very popular from 7pm, serves classic cocktails as well as a varied menu of apéritifs and liqueurs. You can also sit in the bar for a quick lunch. On the first floor, adjoining rooms dedicated to the muses of the "Paéva", "Castiglione" and "Mathilde" eras, as well as the library and chocolate shop with a different atmosphere and decorated in a Second Empire-style by Jacques Garcia.

                                    Accessibility information
                    
        Motor disability: reserved and adapted parking spaces for wheel chair users, ground floor entrance or entrance with sloping surface, low reception desk adapted for wheelchair users, toilet facilities adapted and equipped with transfer bar and sink adapted for wheelchair users, reserved and adapted parking spaces for wheel chair users, dining area with aisles wider than 1.4 metres with adapted tables (height of 70cm so as not to obstruct wheelchair users).
Visual disability: transparent glass doors and walls with contrasting elements, absence of obstacles at face level to impede circulation, stairs with touch-sensitive and visual contrast before the first descending step, with continuous handrails and contrasting stair edges, lift with adapted keypad (raised buttons and/or braille) and with announcement of floor.
Hearing disability: bookings available to do online, reception desk equipped with audio induction loop, lift with visual announcement and/or audio induction loop, flashing lights when fire alarm sounds (in case a person with a hearing disability is alone).
