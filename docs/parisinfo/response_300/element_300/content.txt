                                                    Description
                            
                                
        Inaugurated in 1826 and located between the place de la Concorde and the Louvre Museum, the Greco-Roman-style building was home to the French stock market business for over a century and a half, as the headquarters of the French Stock Exchange. The Palais Brongniart benefits from high level congress equipment thanks to its two auditoriums of 600 and 100 places. Their multiple possibilities of use set them as perfect spaces to host the most significant events of the French capital, in a prestigious business district. Moreover you can also enjoy its legendary spaces such as “La Nef” which is the old trading room, the “Salon d’Honneur” or even the “Espace Corbeille” in order to organize your most beautiful reception up to 1,200 people into this symbolic and history-filled place. In addition, Le Palais Brongniart provides you a vast work area made up of four meeting rooms. These modular rooms can be laid out to fit all your requirements.
