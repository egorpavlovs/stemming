==Organization and finance==
[[Image:Franz Beckenbauer 2006 06 17.jpg|thumb|upright|Bayern's former president from 1994 to 2009 and former player [[Franz Beckenbauer]]]]

{{see also|Most valuable sports teams}}
Bayern is led mostly by former club players. Since 2016,  [[Uli Hoeneß]] serves as the club's president, following [[Karl Hopfner]] who had been in office from 2014; Hoeneß had resigned in 2014 after being convicted of tax fraud.<ref name="Uli Hoeness resigns as Bayern Munich president after court case">{{cite news|title=Uli Hoeness resigns as Bayern Munich president after court case|url=https://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/26574681|accessdate=15 March 2014|newspaper=BBC Sports|date=14 March 2014|deadurl=no|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140315010523/http://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/26574681|archivedate=15 March 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.fcbayern.de/en/club/club/members/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150925081838/https://www.fcbayern.de/en/club/club/members/|archive-date=25 September 2015|title=Members' club|publisher=FC Bayern Munich}}</ref> [[Karl-Heinz Rummenigge]] is the chairman of the executive board of the AG.<ref name="CompanyInfo">{{cite web|title=Company|url=https://www.fcbayern.de/en/club/company/members/|publisher=FC Bayern Munich |accessdate=26 November 2015|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150926003000/http://www.fcbayern.de/en/club/company/members/|archivedate=26 September 2015}}</ref> The [[supervisory board]] of nine consists mostly of managers of big German corporations. Besides the club's president and the board's chairman, they are [[Herbert Hainer]] former CEO of (Adidas), Dr. Herbert Diess chairman of (Volkswagen), Dr. Werner Zedelius senior advisor at (Allianz), [[Timotheus Höttges]] CEO of (Deutsche Telekom), Prof. Dr. Dieter Mayer, [[Edmund Stoiber]], Theodor Weimer CEO of (Deutsche Börse), and Dr. Michael Diederich speaker of the board at (UniCredit Bank).<ref>{{cite journal |year= 2010 |title= Mitglieder des Aufsichtsrates der FC Bayern München AG gewählt |journal= Bayern Magazin |volume= 61 |issue= 11 |page= 14 |language = German}}</ref><ref name="supervisory board members">{{cite web | url = https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2018/12/press-release-new-supervisory-board | title = New FC Bayern Munich AG supervisory board | publisher = fcbayern.com | accessdate = 22 December 2018}}</ref>

Professional football at Bayern is run by the spin-off organization ''FC Bayern München AG''. ''AG'' is short for ''[[Aktiengesellschaft]]'', and Bayern is run like a [[joint stock company]], a company whose stock are not listed on the public stock exchange, but is privately owned. 75% of ''FC Bayern München AG'' is owned by the club, the ''FC Bayern München e.&nbsp;V.'' (''e.&nbsp;V.'' is short for ''[[Eingetragener Verein]]'', which translates into "Registered Club"). Three German corporations, the sports goods manufacturer [[Adidas]], the automobile company [[Audi]] and the financial services group [[Allianz]] each hold 8.33% of the shares, 25% in total.<ref name="CompanyInfo"/> Adidas acquired its shares in 2002 for €77 million. The money was designated to help finance the [[Allianz Arena]].<ref>{{cite book | last = Schulze-Marmeling | first = Dietrich | title = Die Bayern. Die Geschichte des deutschen Rekordmeisters | language = German | publisher = Die Werkstatt | year = 2003 | pages = 473–474 | isbn = 3-89533-426-X}}</ref> In 2009 Audi paid €90 million for their share. The capital was used to repay the loan on the Allianz Arena.<ref name="200809summary">{{cite web|title=FCB in profit for the 17th year in a row |publisher=FC Bayern Munich Official Website |date=27 November 2009 |accessdate=11 December 2009 |url=http://www.fcbayern.t-home.de/en/news/news/2009/21540.php |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091204014012/http://www.fcbayern.t-home.de/en/news/news/2009/21540.php |archivedate=4 December 2009 }}</ref> And in early 2014, Allianz became the third shareholder of the company acquiring theirs share for €110 million. With the sale, Bayern paid off the remaining debt on the Allianz Arena 16 years ahead of schedule.<ref>Allianz buys shares. [https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2014/02/press-release-110214-allianz "Allianz acquires stake in FC Bayern München AG"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180212005250/https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2014/02/press-release-110214-allianz |date=12 February 2018 }}, ''[[fcbayern.com]]'', Munich, 11 February 2014. Retrieved on 11 February 2018.</ref> Bayern's other sports departments are run by the club.
[[File:Testspiel gegen FC Bayern München 03.JPG|thumb|left|The Bayern Munich team bus provided by their sponsor [[MAN SE|MAN]]]]

[[Opel]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Picture of Stefan Effenberg with Opel jersey |publisher=FC Bayern Munich Official Website |url=http://www.fcbayern.t-home.de/en/company/club/hall_of_fame/00147.php Bayern is an exception in professional football, having generated profits for 25 years in a row.<ref>The Swiss Ramble. [http://swissramble.blogspot.is/2016/12/bayern-munich-elevation.html "Bayern Munich – Elevation"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180210235227/http://swissramble.blogspot.is/2016/12/bayern-munich-elevation.html |date=10 February 2018 }}, ''[[The Swiss Ramble]]'', Zürich, 6 December 2016. Retrieved on 10 February 2018.</ref> Other clubs often report losses, realizing transfers via loans, whereas Bayern always uses [[current asset]]s. In the 2018 edition of the [[Deloitte Football Money League]] Bayern had the fourth highest revenue in club football, generating revenue of €587.8 million. Bayern differs from other European top clubs in their income composition. The top 20 European football clubs had an average of 45% of their revenues from broadcasting rights. Bayern earned only 25% of their revenue that way. Bayern have the highest commercial revenue in the 2018 Deloitte Football Money League. Bayern's commercial revenue is at €343.4 million (58% of their total revenue). In contrast, Bayern's Matchday revenue is lacking behind the top clubs at €97.7 million (17% of their total revenue).<ref>2018 Deloitte Football Money League. [https://www2.deloitte.com/content/dam/Deloitte/rs/Documents/consumer-business/Deloitte-Football-Money-League-2018.pdf "2018 Football Money League"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180211071854/https://www2.deloitte.com/content/dam/Deloitte/rs/Documents/consumer-business/Deloitte-Football-Money-League-2018.pdf |date=11 February 2018 }}, ''[[Deloitte.com]]'', London, January 2018. Retrieved on 10 February 2018.</ref>

While other European clubs have mainly marketed to international audiences, Bayern had focused on Germany.<ref>{{cite news | url = https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2012-05-20/bayern-munich-second-in-brand-value-after-champions-league-final.html | title = Bayern Munich Second in Brand Value After Champions League Final | publisher = Bloomberg | date = 21 May 2012 | accessdate = 21 May 2012 | first = Bob | last = Bensch | deadurl = no | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20120524132421/http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2012-05-20/bayern-munich-second-in-brand-value-after-champions-league-final.html | archivedate = 24 May 2012 | df = dmy-all }}</ref> In recent years Bayern have started to focus their marketing more on Asia and the [[United States]]. Bayern made summer tours to the United States in 2014 and 2016. Bayern went to [[China]] in the summer of 2015 and returned in the summer of 2017 where they also played games in [[Singapore]]. In August 2014 Bayern opened an office in New York City as the club wants to strengthen their brand positioning against other top European clubs in the United States.<ref>FCB New York City office. [https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2014/08/fcb-open-office-in-new-york-city "FCB opens New York City office"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180212005230/https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2014/08/fcb-open-office-in-new-york-city |date=12 February 2018 }}, ''[[fcbayern.com]]'', Munich, 1 August 2014. Retrieved on 11 February 2018.</ref> In March 2017, Bayern was the first foreign football club to open an office in mainland China. Bayern hope to attract new sponsors and to increase their merchandising sales. In 2017, [[Forbes]] ranks Bayern as the world's fourth-most valuable football club in their [[Forbes' list of the most valuable football clubs|annual list]], estimating the club's value at €2,500 billion.<ref>Forbes Most Valuable Football Clubs. [https://www.forbes.com/teams/bayern-munich/ "The Business of Soccer"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180210235244/https://www.forbes.com/teams/bayern-munich/ |date=10 February 2018 }}, ''[[Forbes]]'', New York, 1 June 2017. Retrieved on 10 February 2018.</ref>

As a result of Bayern's appearance in the 2012 UEFA Champions League Final, the club's brand value has reached US$786&nbsp;million, up 59% from the previous year. Among European teams, this is ahead of Real Madrid's US$600&nbsp;million and behind first-placed [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]], whose brand is valued at US$853&nbsp;million. In 2013, Bayern overtook Manchester United to take first place in brand valuation.<ref>[http://www.fcbayern.telekom.de/en/news/news/2013/42822.php] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130608001543/http://www.fcbayern.telekom.de/en/news/news/2013/42822.php|date=8 June 2013}}</ref>

Bayern's financial report for the 2017–18 season reported revenue of €657.4 million and an operating profit of €136.5 million. Post-tax profits were €29.5 million which meant that this was Bayern's 26th year in a row with a profit.<ref>Bayern Munich finances. [https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2018/11/agm-financial-results-overview-2017-18 "FC Bayern rests on "very solid foundations"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180210235149/https://fcbayern.com/en/news/2018/11/agm-financial-results-overview-2017-18 |date=30 November 2018 }}, ''[[fcbayern.com]]'', Munich, 30 November 2018. Retrieved on 30 November 2018.</ref>
